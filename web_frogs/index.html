All About Frogs

Frogs are amphibians in the order Anura (meaning "tail-less"), comprising around 88% of extant amphibian species. Frogs are widely distributed, ranging from the tropics to subarctic regions, but the greatest concentration of species diversity is found in tropical rainforests. Frogs are generally characterized by their leaping ability, bulging eyes, and moist skin. They are also an important part of many ecosystems, both as predators and prey.
